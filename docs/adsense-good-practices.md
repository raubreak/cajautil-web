# AdSense and Content Quality Notes

This project applies the following practical rules to improve approval readiness, trust, and long-term monetization quality.

## Applied rules

- Keep public legal pages available and internally linked: privacy, cookies, legal notice, contact, about.
- Avoid absolute privacy claims when analytics or advertising scripts are present.
- Add useful explanatory content around tools instead of publishing widget-only pages.
- Include limits, warnings, and intent clarification on finance, health, and other sensitive calculators.
- Prefer practical examples, FAQs, and related links to strengthen page usefulness.
- Keep structured data consistent with real assets and live URLs.
- Use canonical URLs, robots, sitemap, and ads.txt as baseline technical signals.
- Ensure dynamic URLs (tool variants and articles) generate canonical, Open Graph, and Twitter metadata automatically.
- Reduce internal contradictions between product copy and legal copy.
- Favor local processing when possible, but describe it accurately.
- Use stronger client-side randomness for security-related tools when available.

## Review checklist before requesting AdSense

- ads.txt resolves correctly on production.
- Important pages have enough original text beyond the interface itself.
- Footer and trust pages match actual data practices.
- Contact information is visible and easy to verify.
- No misleading medical, financial, or security claims.
- Internal/admin traffic is excluded from analytics when possible.
- The site is usable on mobile and desktop.

## Next recommended steps

- Review Google Search Console coverage and indexation.
- Keep improving thin pages in batches.
- Expand article coverage for the tool categories with best commercial intent.
- Recheck policy wording whenever analytics or ad setup changes.
